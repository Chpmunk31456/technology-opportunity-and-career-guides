# Project Editorial Quality Standard

Every public document in the Global Technology Opportunities project must pass this review before publication.

## Required review stages

1. **Spelling and grammar**  
   Correct spelling, punctuation, sentence structure, agreement, capitalization, and word choice.

2. **Human voice**  
   Remove repetitive, mechanical, overly formal, or generic wording. Preserve warmth, dignity, encouragement, and the author's purpose.

3. **Plain language**  
   Explain unfamiliar terms, shorten unnecessarily long sentences, and avoid jargon when a clearer phrase works.

4. **Technical accuracy**  
   Verify dates, eligibility, compensation, program status, and official links. Clearly separate verified facts from guidance or opinion.

5. **Accessibility**  
   Use structured headings, meaningful link text, readable tables, alt text for informative images, and accessible file formats.

6. **Ethical review**  
   Avoid guarantees, inflated claims, hidden costs, discriminatory language, and instructions that may expose applicants to fraud or harm.

7. **Visual quality assurance**  
   Render the final DOCX and PDF, inspect every page, and correct clipping, broken links, inconsistent spacing, or layout problems.

8. **Final read-aloud pass**  
   Read important sections aloud to identify awkward phrasing that automated grammar tools may miss.

## Use of grammar tools

Tools such as Microsoft Editor or Grammarly may be used as a second opinion, but suggestions must never be accepted automatically. Automated tools can weaken a personal voice, change technical meaning, or remove intentional warmth. The final editorial decision must always be made in context.

## Publication rule

A document may be labeled **Final** only after it passes all eight stages and its factual information has a visible verification date.
