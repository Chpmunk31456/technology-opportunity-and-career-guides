# Technology Career Guides

Free, practical career roadmaps designed to help people explore, prepare for, and enter technology careers.

This section is part of the broader **Technology Opportunity and Career Guides** project. It connects career exploration with free learning resources, practical tools, portfolio projects, internships, apprenticeships, fellowships, accessibility guidance, and human-centered support.

## What Each Career Guide Includes

Each roadmap is designed to explain:

- What the role does
- Common responsibilities
- Entry-level and advanced job titles
- Core technical skills
- Important communication and human skills
- Degree and non-degree pathways
- Free learning resources
- Free and open-source tools
- Portfolio project ideas
- GitHub evidence
- Internship, apprenticeship, fellowship, and mentorship pathways
- Certification options
- Resume and interview preparation
- Accessibility considerations
- Ethical, privacy, cybersecurity, and legal responsibilities
- A practical 30-, 60-, and 90-day learning plan

## Career Guidance Disclaimer

This material supports education and career exploration. It is not psychological testing, medical advice, legal advice, rehabilitation evaluation, or licensed counseling.

Personality and interest tools describe preferences, not intelligence, ability, or destiny. No job, degree, salary, certification, or family expectation is more important than a person’s safety and life.

If career pressure is contributing to hopelessness or thoughts of self-harm, stop career planning and seek immediate help from a trusted person, qualified professional, local emergency service, or crisis service.

## Project Mission

Many people have the ability to make a meaningful difference in the world, but they lack money, guidance, accommodations, connections, or access to reliable information.

Our goal is to help at least one person use technology to change the world for the better—and encourage that person to help someone else.

**Help one person. Share one verified opportunity. Teach one useful skill. Leave the path clearer than you found it.**
