# Top 50 Technology Careers of the Future

This guide will help learners, students, career changers, and professionals understand technology careers that are likely to remain important as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, cloud computing, data, automation, accessibility, and digital infrastructure continue to grow.

The final guide will include:

- A plain-language description of each career
- Typical responsibilities
- Entry-level and advanced job titles
- Required technical and nontechnical skills
- Degree and non-degree pathways
- Free learning resources
- Recommended certifications
- Portfolio projects
- GitHub evidence
- Internship, apprenticeship, fellowship, and mentorship pathways
- Resume and interview guidance
- Accessibility considerations
- Ethical and legal responsibilities
- Long-term career progression

## Career Categories

1. Artificial Intelligence and Intelligent Systems
2. Cybersecurity, Privacy, and Digital Trust
3. Data, Cloud, and Software Platforms
4. Robotics, Infrastructure, and Emerging Technology
5. Accessibility, Assistive Technology, and Human-Centered Technology

## Project Status

This guide is currently under research and development.

All career information will be reviewed for:

- Current labor-market relevance
- Free and low-cost learning access
- Global applicability
- Accessibility
- Ethical and legal considerations
- Human-readable language
- Verified official sources

## Mission

Many people have the ability to make a meaningful difference in the world, but they lack money, guidance, accommodations, connections, or access to reliable information.

Our goal is to help at least one person use technology to change the world for the better—and encourage that person to help someone else.

**Help one person. Share one verified opportunity. Teach one useful skill. Leave the path clearer than you found it.**
