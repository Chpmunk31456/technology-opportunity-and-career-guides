# How to Build an Identity and Access Management Program

HOW TO BUILD AN IDENTITY AND ACCESS MANAGEMENT PROGRAM

A concise, practical implementation manual

Alberto (Al) Leiva

Version 1.0 - July 2026

## Purpose

Use this manual for lawful, authorized, defensive work. Adapt controls to organizational risk, legal obligations, accessibility needs, and available resources.

## Program foundations

- Treat identity as a security control and a business capability.
- Define authoritative sources, identity types, ownership, policies, assurance needs, and lifecycle events.
- Include employees, contractors, customers, partners, workloads, devices, service accounts, APIs, and AI agents.
## Joiner, mover, and leaver lifecycle

- Create access from approved business roles and verified identity data.
- Update access promptly when duties change.
- Disable access, sessions, tokens, certificates, and physical credentials at departure.
## Authentication

- Use phishing-resistant MFA for privileged and high-risk access.
- Apply risk-based authentication and secure recovery procedures.
- Protect enrollment, authenticator replacement, and help-desk resets against social engineering.
## Authorization and least privilege

- Use role-based and attribute-based controls where appropriate.
- Separate incompatible duties and require approval for sensitive entitlements.
- Use just-in-time and time-limited privileged access.
## Privileged and machine identities

- Vault privileged credentials and monitor administrative sessions.
- Inventory service accounts, secrets, certificates, keys, workloads, and non-human identities.
- Assign owners, rotate credentials, restrict use, and remove abandoned identities.
## Access reviews and governance

- Review high-risk access more frequently.
- Give reviewers clear context and require evidence-based decisions.
- Track orphaned accounts, excessive access, toxic combinations, failed removals, and exceptions.
## Federation, privacy, and roadmap

- Validate trust, claims, signing keys, redirect targets, session controls, and partner responsibilities.
- Minimize identity data and provide accessible authentication options.
- Days 1-30: inventory and leaver controls. Days 31-60: MFA, privileged access, and reviews. Days 61-90: automation, machine identity, metrics, and testing.
