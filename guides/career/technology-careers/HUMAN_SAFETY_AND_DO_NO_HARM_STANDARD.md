# Human Safety and Do No Harm Standard

**Project principle:** We shall not do harm.

No career outcome, job offer, certification, degree, salary, or family expectation is more important than a person's safety, dignity, and life.

## Required practices

- Do not shame people for unemployment, disability, age, education, language, financial hardship, rejection, or career gaps.
- Do not promise employment, income, or acceptance.
- Present multiple pathways and permission to pause or change direction.
- Protect personal information and collect only what is necessary.
- Separate career guidance from licensed mental-health, medical, legal, financial, immigration, or rehabilitation services.
- Use strengths-based language without hiding real requirements.
- Make accommodations and accessibility part of planning from the beginning.
- Stop career planning when immediate safety may be at risk.

## Safety message for every major guide

> Your value is not determined by a job title, salary, degree, certification, interview, or application result. A setback may require another path, more time, support, or recovery. It does not mean that you failed your family or that your life has lost its purpose.

## When someone may be at risk

Take statements about self-harm, being a burden, having no reason to live, or others being better off without them seriously. Listen without judgment. Encourage immediate contact with a trusted person and qualified local support. When danger appears imminent, contact local emergency services or another appropriate emergency resource.

This repository will not publish guessed crisis numbers. Local crisis information must be verified for the reader's country and reviewed regularly.
