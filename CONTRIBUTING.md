# Contributing to Cybersecurity Career and Practical Guides

Thank you for helping improve this public educational collection.

## Helpful Contributions

- Factual corrections
- Broken or outdated links
- Accessibility improvements
- English or Spanish terminology corrections
- Clearer beginner explanations
- Updates to standards, frameworks, products, certifications, or official guidance
- Ethical portfolio and learning-project ideas

## Before Submitting

1. Identify the guide, language, version, format, and affected section.
2. Describe the problem and proposed correction.
3. Cite primary or authoritative sources whenever possible.
4. Explain whether the change affects other language or file-format editions.
5. Confirm that the submission contains no confidential, proprietary, personal, regulated, assessment, client, or production data.

## Safety and Integrity Requirements

Contributions must not:

- Enable unauthorized access, disruption, persistence, surveillance, credential theft, or data collection
- Include credentials, private keys, tokens, personal information, client data, or production details
- Fabricate employment history, certifications, education, projects, or professional achievements
- Promise employment, certification, compliance, security, salary, or other guaranteed outcomes
- Misrepresent legal, regulatory, audit, certification, or professional requirements
- Reproduce third-party material without permission

Security-testing material must be limited to lawful, authorized, and clearly scoped environments.

## Translation Guidance

- Use natural, locale-appropriate professional language.
- Preserve technical meaning, limitations, warnings, and ethical safeguards.
- Identify whether a fluent human reviewer checked the submission.
- Do not describe machine-assisted translation as human-reviewed.

## Accessibility Guidance

Preserve logical headings, meaningful links, real lists, correct table headers, alternative text, contrast, reading order, document-language settings, and clear language. See [ACCESSIBILITY.md](ACCESSIBILITY.md).

## Issues and Pull Requests

For an issue, include:

- Guide and language
- Version and format
- Page or section
- Problem description
- Proposed correction
- Supporting source

Keep pull requests focused. Explain what changed, why it changed, which evidence supports it, and how it was checked.

Do not include sensitive information in public issues or pull requests.
