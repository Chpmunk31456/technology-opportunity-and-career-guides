# Technology Opportunity and Career Guides

Free global resources helping people move from learning to opportunity.

This project connects people with:

- Employment opportunities
- Internships
- Apprenticeships
- Fellowships
- Mentorship programs
- Graduate and trainee programs
- Free educational resources
- Technology career guides
- Portfolio and GitHub guidance
- Resume and interview preparation
- Accessibility and accommodation support
- Job-scam prevention
- Human-centered career guidance

## Mission

Many people have the ability to make a meaningful difference in the world, but they lack money, guidance, accommodations, connections, or access to reliable information.

This project exists to help at least one person use technology to change the world for the better—and to encourage that person to help someone else.

## Project Principles

### Lift as You Rise

Another person’s success does not reduce our own.

We encourage everyone who benefits from this project to help one other person by:

- Sharing a verified opportunity
- Explaining an application requirement
- Reviewing a beginner’s project
- Translating a resource
- Improving accessibility
- Reporting an expired link
- Offering encouragement after rejection

### Human Safety Comes First

No job, degree, salary, certification, or family expectation is more important than a person’s safety, dignity, and life.

This project does not shame people for unemployment, disability, age, education, career gaps, or financial hardship.

If career pressure is contributing to hopelessness or thoughts of self-harm, career planning should stop and immediate support from a trusted person, qualified professional, local emergency service, or crisis service should take priority.

### We Shall Not Do Harm

We will:

- Encourage without pressuring
- Guide without judging
- Inform without misleading
- Protect privacy
- Avoid false promises
- Respect disability, culture, age, education, and circumstance
- Recognize when career guidance should stop and immediate human support should begin

## Editorial and Quality Standard

Every document in this project should be reviewed for:

- Spelling and grammar
- Punctuation
- Natural human voice
- Plain language
- Factual accuracy
- Accessibility
- Ethical guidance
- Privacy and safety
- Working official links
- Visual quality

## Community Commitment

We do not support gatekeeping, ridicule, false promises, exploitation, plagiarism, or deliberate discouragement.

We believe in sharing useful information, correcting errors respectfully, recognizing contributors, and helping people move forward.

**Help one person. Share one verified opportunity. Teach one useful skill. Leave the path clearer than you found it.**
